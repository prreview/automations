# Company: Tetsuwan Scientific
- **Description**: Tetsuwan Scientific builds ResearchOS, a scientific-native language layer for lab automation. A scientist writes a protocol in plain language, ResearchOS turns it into robot code, and the team can inspect or edit the decisions before anything runs. The company is in pilot mode, with a first system already running at a rare disease RNA therapeutics company. The public blog says the team is 7 people, and the hiring post says it includes 3 software engineers, 1 automation engineer, the founders, and a designer soon. Public revenue is not spelled out, but the shape is clear enough, software sales into labs plus deployment and support work as customers move experiments onto the system. They sit next to Opentrons, Benchling, PyLabRobot, and UniteLabs, but the wedge is the interface between scientists and robots, not a generic lab tool. They also raised an oversubscribed pre-seed round led by 2048 Ventures, with Carbon Silicon, Everywhere, Referent Ventures, 100 Plus Capital, Neman Ventures, Transpose Platform, and Entrepreneur First in the mix. That gives them a decent cushion for a tiny team, though burn is not public.
