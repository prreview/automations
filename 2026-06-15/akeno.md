# Company: akeno
- **Description**: akeno builds production planning and scheduling software for process manufacturers. The product sits inside customer networks, connects to ERP, MES, DCS, and shopfloor systems, and keeps schedules updated as conditions change. Their site says the software is used by BASF Coatings and Sun Chemical, which tells me they already have enterprise deployments in the wild, not just a slide deck.

  The business looks like a classic B2B industrial software sale. Revenue is likely a mix of software subscription plus implementation and support, since the careers pages talk about customer locations, go-live support, and ongoing operations. I could not find a public funding announcement, so I would treat this as either bootstrapped or quietly funded. Runway is not public. Still, 22 employees on LinkedIn and live hiring suggest the company is active and has enough cash to keep building.

  Tech-wise, the stack is visible on the careers and tech pages: React, Nest.js, PostgreSQL, Docker compose, Helm in progress, and a lot of integration work around OT and IT systems. The product is narrow in domain and heavy on operational detail, which usually means the team cares more about reliability and customer fit than flashy demos.
