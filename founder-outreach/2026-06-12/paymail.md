# Company: Paymail
- **Description**: Paymail is a very early fintech product that wants to make invoicing and money transfer work through email addresses instead of bank branch and account numbers. The current wedge is email-native payments: send money to `email@example.org`, receive invoices by email, and use banking rails behind the scenes. The product is still pre-MVP, with a medium-sized React and TypeScript codebase already written by the founder. Revenue is likely transaction fees, invoice tooling fees, or a paid business tier once the product reaches pilot users. Funding is not announced; the HN post says the role is startup equity only, so runway looks founder-funded and lean. The closest comparisons are x402-style internet-native payments, Wise-style transfer UX, and invoicing tools that hide payment complexity for small businesses. This is a high-risk fit, but the ownership is unusually high: Raj would be joining before MVP, shaping UX, frontend architecture, and payment flows from the first usable release.
