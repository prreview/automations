# Company: Charge Robotics
- **Description**: Charge Robotics builds robots that assemble and place solar farm sections on site. The company is aimed at utility-scale solar, where crews still spend months on repetitive hand work. MIT covered it in March 2025 and described the product as a portable factory that automatically assembles and installs completed sections of solar farms. YC lists 25 employees and the company says it has shipped its first commercial system already. The Ashby page says Charge is a Series A startup, backed by Founders Fund, Energy Impact Partners, Lux Capital, YC (S21), and more. The money path looks like hardware, deployment, and service sold to solar developers and EPCs. Exact runway is not public, but the mix of 25 people, live deployments, and a Series A stage says this is already past the demo stage.
